About Me

Hi — I’m Ben, aka BenGurWaves. I’m 16, self-funded, and stubborn enough to call music my life’s work. This isn’t a hobby I do between school and snacks. It’s the way I make sense of the world, the way I breathe when everything else gets loud.

Early life & musical roots

Piano found me at six. I moved through teachers until one of them introduced me to the Royal Conservatory — slow, steady, exacting lessons that taught me discipline and love for detail. Ten years later, I’m still learning, still hungry, and still trying to translate feeling into sound.

Saxophone

I picked up sax in third grade when the school orchestra dragged me in (in the best way). I kept at it, worked with a coach who pushed my phrasing and tone, and the sax became a voice I return to when I want breath, bend, and phrasing that talks like a person.

Family & roots

Music isn’t an interest in my house — it’s the language. Parents, grandparents, even great-grandparents made noise and harmony before me. I didn’t choose music so much as inherit it. That inheritance is messy, loud, tender, and I’m grateful for every note.

Guitar

Guitar came later. I tried a few teachers and ended up with years of focused practice — not to show off, but because I like the way nylon strings change a song’s mood. Right now I play mostly to myself, but I’ll fold guitar into releases when the piece needs that warm, intimate voice.

Violin

Violin was a challenge I chased because my sister played and I like things other people call “impossible.” Two, maybe three years of fiddly practice taught me patience: bow control, small adjustments, and the stubbornness to keep showing up after squeaks and missed notes. I didn’t master it — not even close — but I learned how to sit with slow progress and not quit. That stubbornness shows up everywhere now.

My four main instruments

Piano · Saxophone · Guitar · Violin

The push (and the pause)

When I started releasing music I lied to myself about why I was doing it. I told myself it wasn’t about the numbers — but it was. I was running a weekly sprint: record, scramble to mix, post a teaser, upload, release. I was burning out, chasing momentum. Summer 2025 changed everything. I hit pause, learned the craft properly — writing, mixing, producing — and realized growth doesn’t end. It just keeps asking for more patience.

How I write

Piano is my home base — the clearest way I hear ideas. But I don’t fence myself to one instrument: some songs start as a chord, others as a rhythm, sometimes as a single line of melody on sax. I don’t obsess over theory first; I chase feeling. The technical stuff comes after, to shape what the heart already said.

I’m inspired by people who make music feel like landscape: Hans Zimmer, Gibran Alcocer, Tony Anderson, Jordan Critz. I listen to how they build space and atmosphere, then try to plant my own small lights inside that dark. I want to get there — not for clout, but because that kind of depth is what I’m reaching for.

Faith & honesty

My faith shapes how I move, but I’m not a caricature of perfect. I stumble. I make terrible mixes. I mess up in front of people. The music is how I work through it — the doubt, the joy, the question, the praise. I give credit where it’s due, but I’m also real: I fail, I laugh, I try again.

Life off the grid

I like solitude, long walks in nature, and quiet rooms for composing. Big crowds aren’t my vibe — I’d pick a late night of writing over a party. But put me with a close friend for an afternoon and I’ll trade the studio for laughter and terrible jokes any day. Travel, silence, and real conversation feed the songs I want to write.

Thank you for listening — for taking a minute to read my messy, honest story. I hope the music gives you a place to breathe. That’s the goal, always.

"All glory be to God. I am nothing without Him, and my music is nothing either."
